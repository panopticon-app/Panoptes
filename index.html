›
  • 91 days Maximum to issue FPN from date of offence
  • 29 days Discount payment window from date FPN issued

📋 FPN Issue Checklist

›
  • 1. Confirm offence and section reference
  • 2. Check 91-day window has not expired
  • 3. Identify the correct undertaker / permit holder
  • 4. Take photographic evidence of the breach
  • 5. Record exact location, USRN, coordinates if possible
  • 6. Note works reference / permit reference
  • 7. Issue FPN — original to undertaker, copy retained
  • 8. Log in your authority's FPN register

NRSWA 1991 — Statutory Sections

📖 s.54 — Advance notice of works

›
💬 Plain English

Before a utility company digs up the road they must warn the council in advance. For most jobs that's at least 10 working days' notice. For big jobs it's 3 months. Only genuine emergencies are exempt. If they just start digging without telling anyone — that's this offence.

NRSWA 1991 s.54
Advance notice of certain works

(1) Before beginning to execute any street works to which this section applies, the undertaker shall give the street authority such advance notice as may be prescribed of his intention to execute the works.

(2) This section applies to street works which are not emergency works.

(3) The notice shall be given not less than the prescribed period before the works begin.

Minimum notice: 10 working days (standard), 3 months (major works). Emergency works exempt — s.57 applies instead.
⚠️ FPN: £2,500 full / £1,875 discounted (pay within 29 days)

📖 s.55 — Notice of actual start date

›
💬 Plain English

Even after giving advance notice the company must confirm the actual date they're starting. If they turn up earlier than they said — or move the job forward without telling the council — that's this offence. Think of it as the second notification: "we told you we were coming, now here's the exact date."

NRSWA 1991 s.55
Notice of starting date of works

(1) An undertaker who has given notice under section 54 shall give the street authority notice of the date on which the works are to begin, not less than the prescribed period before that date.

(2) If the works are not begun on the notified date, the undertaker shall give fresh notice under this section.

(3) An undertaker who begins works without giving notice as required by this section, or before the notified date, is guilty of an offence and liable to a fixed penalty notice.

Commonly triggered when a job is brought forward without updating the street authority. A fresh notice is required any time the start date changes.
⚠️ FPN: £2,500 full / £1,875 discounted (pay within 29 days)

📖 s.57 — Notice of emergency works

›
💬 Plain English

If there's a burst pipe or cable emergency the company can start digging straight away — that's fine. But they still have to phone the council as soon as they possibly can after starting and explain why it's an emergency. Forgetting to call, or doing it hours later with no good reason, is this offence.

NRSWA 1991 s.57
Notice of emergency works

(1) An undertaker who executes emergency works shall, as soon as reasonably practicable after commencing the works, give the street authority notice of— (a) the place and nature of the works, and (b) the reason for treating the works as emergency works.

(2) An undertaker who fails to comply with subsection (1) is guilty of an offence and liable to a fixed penalty notice.

In practice notice is expected within 2 hours of commencement. The notice must state the specific reason — a vague response is not sufficient.
⚠️ FPN: £2,500 full / £1,875 discounted (pay within 29 days)

📖 s.59 — Duty to reinstate

›
💬 Plain English

Once the work is done the company must put the road back to at least the same standard it was before — properly, using the right materials and methods. They can't just dump some tarmac in the hole and leave. If the patch is badly done, too thin, wrong mix, or they never get round to the permanent fix — that's this offence.

NRSWA 1991 s.59
General duty of undertaker to reinstate

(1) An undertaker who executes street works shall reinstate the street to at least its condition before the works were executed (or better condition), and shall do so in accordance with any directions given to him by the street authority and the prescribed specifications and standards.

(2) The undertaker shall complete reinstatement within the prescribed period, and the duty to maintain the reinstatement continues for the prescribed guarantee period.

(3) An undertaker who fails to comply with this section is guilty of an offence and liable to a fixed penalty notice.

Standards set by SROH. Permanent reinstatement: 6 months (bound) or 3 months (unbound). Guarantee period: 2–3 years.
⚠️ FPN: £2,500 full / £1,875 discounted. Multiple FPNs possible for multiple openings.

📖 s.60 — Safety at the worksite

›
💬 Plain English

While work is going on the site must be made safe for everyone — pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers. Proper barriers, signs, and lights around any holes or hazards, set up correctly to the national code. If you arrive and there are no cones, no signs, an open trench with nothing round it, or the traffic management is a shambles — that's this offence.

NRSWA 1991 s.60
Safety measures

(1) An undertaker executing street works shall— (a) secure that the works are properly guarded and lit in accordance with the prescribed requirements, and (b) comply with such requirements as may be prescribed as to the use of traffic signs in connection with the works.

(2) An undertaker who fails to comply with subsection (1) is guilty of an offence and liable to a fixed penalty notice.

Requirements set by Safety at Street Works Code of Practice and Chapter 8 of the Traffic Signs Manual. Applies for the entire duration of works.
⚠️ FPN: £2,500 full / £1,875 discounted. Multiple FPNs possible. See also NCT Condition C1 (£500).

📖 s.70 — Ignoring council directions

›
💬 Plain English

The council can tell a utility company how and when to do their work — for example "you can only work between 9pm and 6am on this high street." If the company ignores a valid instruction and carries on regardless that's this offence. The council must have properly issued the direction first — verbal requests don't count.

NRSWA 1991 s.70
Directions as to timing and placing of works

(1) The street authority may give directions to an undertaker as to the time at which, or the manner in which, street works are to be executed, if the authority consider it necessary to do so— (a) for the purpose of preventing or reducing damage to the street, (b) preventing or reducing inconvenience to the public, or (c) in the interests of safety.

(2) An undertaker who fails to comply with a direction given under this section is guilty of an offence and liable to a fixed penalty notice.

Direction must be validly issued and formally served before this FPN can be used. Retain a copy of the direction as evidence.
⚠️ FPN: £2,500 full / £1,875 discounted (pay within 29 days)

📖 s.74 — Failure to notify completion

›
💬 Plain English

When a job is finished the company must tell the council straight away. The council uses this notice to start the clock on reinstatement guarantees and to update their street works register. Forgetting to send the completion notice, or sending it weeks late, is this offence. Note: staying on site too long is a separate issue — that triggers financial charges, not this FPN.

NRSWA 1991 s.74
Charges where works unreasonably prolonged

(1) The Secretary of State may make regulations providing for undertakers to be liable to pay charges to the street authority where street works are not completed within the agreed period.

(2) An undertaker shall also give the street authority notice as soon as the works are completed.

(3) An undertaker who fails to give notice as required is guilty of an offence and liable to a fixed penalty notice.

The FPN offence is failure to give completion notice — not overrunning. Overrunning triggers separate financial charges under SI 2009/303.
⚠️ FPN (completion notice only): £2,500 full / £1,875 discounted. Overrunning charges are separate.
